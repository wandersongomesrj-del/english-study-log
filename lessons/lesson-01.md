# Lesson 01: Pronunciation Hacks 🗣️

In this lesson, I learned about **Common Contractions** to sound more natural and fluent.

### 1. Common Contractions (Verb to Be)
* **I am** → I'm  /aɪm/
* **You are** → You're /jʊər/
* **That is** → That's /ðæts/

### 2. Fast Questions
* **What's wrong?** (Instead of "What is wrong?")
* **What're you doing?** (Instead of "What are you doing?")

### 3. Informal/Regional Variations (Bonus)
* **Y'all're** (You all are - very common in the Southern US)
* **Guys're** (The guys are)

### 💡 My Practice Sentence:
"I'm learning English because I want to be a global developer!"


### 🗣️ Pronunciation Examples
1. **I think I’m in love.**
   (Eu acho que estou apaixonado.)
   *Pronunciation hint:* Focus on the connection between "think" and "I'm".

2. **You’re smarter than I am.**
   (Você é mais esperto(a) do que eu.)
   *Quick Tip:* Notice we use "You're" at the start, but "I am" at the end. 
   We don't say "than I'm".

3. **I’m sure we’re lost.**
   (Eu tenho certeza de que estamos perdidos.)
   *Two contractions here:* **I'm** and **we're**.

4. **They’re glad you’re here.**
   (Eles/Elas estão felizes que você está aqui.)
   *Focus:* "They’re" and "you’re".

5. **He’s upset because she’s leaving.**
   (Ele está chateado porque ela está indo embora.)
   *Watch out:* Two third-person contractions (**He's** and **She's**).

6. **That’s why it’s hard sometimes.**
   (É por isso que às vezes é difícil.)
   *Focus:* "That's" (That is) and "it's" (it is).
